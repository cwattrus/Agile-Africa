Agile Africa Speakers

Martin Fowler

USA

Tweet

I am an author, speaker... essentially a loud-mouthed pundit on the topic of software development. I've been working in the software industry since the mid-80's where I got into the then-new world of object-oriented software. I spent much of the 90's as a consultant and trainer helping people develop object-oriented systems, with a focus on enterprise applications. In 2000 I joined ThoughtWorks.

My main interest is to understand how to design software systems, so as to maximize the productivity of development teams. In doing this I've looked to understand the patterns of good software design, and also the processes that support software design. I've become a big fan of agile approaches and the resulting focus on evolutionary software design.

Annu Augustine

South Africa

Tweet
Annu Augustine has 14 years of experience in the software development arena. She started her career programming in C++ and has worked in waterfall, iterative and agile environments. She is an ardent supporter of agile processes because of its focus on the customer and emphasis on delivering value to the customer in the shortest possible time.

Annu has been involved in various agile conferences and is the producer of Agile Product Management stage at Agile India 2012.

Annu has developed a great appreciation for good products and everything involved in transforming ideas into valuable products. She is an advocate for simplicity and strives to remove complexity in software products. She has an affinity for technology and passionate about using technology to solve relevant problems. She has lots of enthusiasm for product management because it lives at the intersection of product, people and processes, a perfect blend!

Kim Johnston

Tweet
Kim Johnston is a Director at SQS South Africa with 20+ years' IT experience gained across a broad range of industries in SA, the UK & USA. Samantha Laing

South Africa

Tweet
I started my career as a developer. Attention to detail, finding and fixing bugs, and also communicating well to managers and customers quickly saw me promoted to Team Lead and then to Development manager. It was in this position, on a death march project, that I realized there had to be another way and recalled a pleasant project I worked on as a developer – a scrum project. After much reading, and applying some changes, I realized that I wanted to be a scrum master and started my new agile career.

Now, a few years later I find myself guiding and mentoring others with a passion for agile. I strongly believe that everyone should enjoy and love what they do, which is why my day job at Growing Agile is all about my passion of growing agile people. I strive to make the entire process from business vision to development less frustrating and more rewarding for everyone involved. I have spoken at some large Agile conferences and met some amazing people worldwide.

My personal motto is 'be brave', and I embody this by taking on challenges one small step at a time. On weekends you can find me trail running on a mountain or being lazy on the couch with my family.

Dr. Ernest Mnkandla

South Africa

Tweet
Ernest is a senior lecturer at the University of Johannesburg, South Africa in the Department of Applied Information Systems, he lectures Programming in Java, Software Engineering and IT Project Management. He has lectured in ICT for more than eleven years. Ernest's educational background includes an honours degree in Electrical Engineering, a master degree in Computer Science, and a PhD in Software Engineering. He is a Certified Scrum Master.

Ernest's research interests are in Software engineering (specifically these sub-disciplines of Software Engineering; Software Engineering Management, Software Development Process, Software Engineering Tools). Ernest investigates the following areas; Software Project Management (tools and techniques), the relations between PMI's PMBOK and Agile Project Management, the adoption issues in agile software development methods, the broader issues of software process improvement (SPI), and the application of predictive analytics in project management intelligence.

He is passionate about improving the quality of software development at an individual developer's level i.e. the Personal Software Process (PSP) and he coaches young developers to measure and improve their performance. Ernest has presented extensively on agile methodologies and software development project management within Africa and internationally.
